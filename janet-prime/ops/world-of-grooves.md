# World of Grooves

## What It Is

World of Grooves is Denver's fine art practice — his primary artistic identity and long-term primary business.

The medium is vinyl records — transformed into sculptural artwork, large-scale installations, and detailed portraits of iconic musicians and cultural figures.

The brand sits at the intersection of music culture, visual art, and luxury collectibles.

## Work and Medium

**Portraits:** Detailed vinyl record portraits of iconic artists
- Completed portfolio: Elton John (client: Mike), James Brown, David Bowie (Ziggy Stardust), Aretha Franklin, Bob Marley, Jim Morrison, Major Tom, Patti Smith — several submitted to Beverly Hills Art Show (Spring 2026)
- Mixed-media 2D and sculptural artwork using meticulously cut and reassembled vinyl records
- Commission pricing: $2,500 minimum per portrait; pairs commissioned at $5,000–$9,000 depending on size and materials
- Size tiers for two-subject commissions: 26x39, 30x45, 34x51 inches; available in black vinyl or with secondary materials
- Client intake via GoHighLevel form with conditional logic — qualifies clients and sets expectations before consultation
- AI illustration tools (Nano Banana / Higgs Field) generate ~75–85% accurate style-matched portrait references, reducing illustration time; framed to clients as "photo restoration software" — never disclosed as AI tool names

**Sculpture:** Large-scale sculptural installations from vinyl records
- **Echo of Emergence:** 6.5-ft butterfly sculpture — 2025 Burning Man Honoraria Grant recipient. Price floor established at a significant figure; sets the baseline for all major commissions. Do not negotiate below this.
- **Peace Sign (in development):** 10-foot peace sign hand gesture covered in heat-formed vinyl records; tiered strategy starting with smaller proof-of-concept pieces before investing in large-format equipment
- **Ergonomic infrastructure:** Overhead support systems are a real production requirement as piece sizes scale up — not a luxury consideration

**Fabrication tools:** Bambu Labs P2S FDM printer, Anycubic Photon Mono X resin printer, DeWalt 60-gal air compressor, Iwata airbrush, makerspace (CNC, laser, waterjet); foam rollers and studio fabrication tools

## Technology Integration (Future)

Denver has explored embedding AR and NFC experiences into artwork — allowing viewers to hold a phone up to a piece and see it "come to life."

Reference platform: **Ixkio** — being studied as a model for NFC authentication/content-gating.

Target use cases:
- NFC tags embedded in finished pieces for authentication, creation process video, location and reference imagery
- Content-gating system for musicians and fine artists (longer-term platform vision)
- QR/wayfinding to deepen collector experience post-sale
