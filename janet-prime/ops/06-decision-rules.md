# Decision Rules

Use these frameworks when evaluating specific projects and inquiries. For broader opportunity evaluation, see `decision-framework.md`.

---

## Project Decision Matrix (Plume Creative)

| Budget | Industry Fit | Portfolio Potential | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| $20K+ | Any | Any | **ALWAYS YES** |
| $15–20K | Any | Any | **YES** |
| $10–15K | Hospitality | High | YES |
| $10–15K | Corporate | Low | PROBABLY YES |
| $5–10K | Hospitality | High | YES |
| $5–10K | Corporate | Low | MAYBE (if fast/easy) |
| <$5K | Hospitality | High | RARE EXCEPTION |
| <$5K | Corporate | Any | **NO** |

**Key principle:** Not every project needs to be a portfolio piece. A $20K medical rebrand should be accepted because revenue enables selectivity elsewhere. Portfolio curation happens after completion — show hospitality and culturally interesting work publicly; keep corporate revenue work private.

---

## Client Red Flags (When to Decline)

- "Just need a logo" with no brand strategy interest
- Price shopping multiple designers for the lowest bid
- Same-day or extremely rushed timelines
- Committee-driven approval process with no single decision authority
- Client wants to art-direct the creative process themselves
- Already designed everything, just needs execution
- Budget focused entirely on buildout with "whatever's left" for branding

---

## Proposal Rules

**Never send a proposal before confirming:**
1. Budget range (are they in the right tier?)
2. Decision-maker (who has final authority?)
3. Timeline (is there a real deadline?)

If any of these are unknown, the next step is a qualifying conversation — not a proposal.

---

## Opportunity Evaluation Questions

Before committing time or energy to any opportunity:

1. Does this align with the $150K short-term or $200K–$500K long-term revenue goal?
2. Does this strengthen Plume Creative or World of Grooves positioning?
3. Does this give Denver direct client access, or is it intermediary-only?
4. Can the scope be clearly defined and protected?
5. Does this require more setup/infrastructure before it can be executed?
6. Is this the right time, given current deadlines?

## BNI Referral Tiers

**Bread & Butter (frequent, weekly):**
Menus, basic collateral, logo updates, print materials — $2–5K range. Keeps cash flowing.

**Cream (high-value, a few per year):**
Full brand identity for new restaurant/hotel, established business rebrand — $15–30K range.

**Dream (life-changing):**
Casino or resort brand development with retainer, Hard Rock art installation, national hospitality group rebrand, luxury hotel creative director role.

- Custom pieces in $5K–$12K range for hospitality venue placement

## When to Say No

Say no (or defer) when:
- The budget doesn't clear the matrix
- The client shows red flags
- The project would dilute Plume Creative's positioning
- The scope can't be adequately defined before starting
- Pursuing it requires deprioritizing a current deadline
- It positions Denver as an order-taker rather than a creative partner
