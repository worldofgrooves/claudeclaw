**Standard Operating Procedure: Client Onboarding at Plume Creative**

**1\. Introduction**

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the process for onboarding new branding clients at Plume Creative. It is designed to ensure a consistent, premium experience for all clients, from initial contact through project initiation, while effectively gathering necessary information and establishing clear project scopes and pricing. Plume Creative specializes in developing bespoke brand identities for hospitality, entertainment, and lifestyle brands, transforming businesses into immersive experiences through conceptual design that commands attention and evokes emotion.

**2\. Client Interaction & Initial Contact**

The initial interaction with a potential client sets the tone for the entire project. Plume Creative aims to provide a seamless, professional, and engaging experience from the very first touchpoint.

**2.1. Inquiry Reception**

All client inquiries, whether via website form, email, or referral, should be directed to the designated Client Relations Manager. The Client Relations Manager is responsible for logging the inquiry in the CRM system and sending an initial acknowledgment within 24 business hours.

**2.2. Initial Qualification & Information Request**

Upon acknowledgment, the Client Relations Manager will send a brief introductory email that includes:

* A warm welcome to Plume Creative.

* A concise overview of Plume Creative's unique approach and specialization in hospitality, entertainment, and lifestyle brands. This can be a link to a beautifully designed capabilities deck.

* A link to a brief online questionnaire or a request for specific preliminary information (e.g., company name, industry, project goals, estimated timeline, budget range, key challenges they are facing, and what they hope to achieve with a new brand identity). This helps in understanding the client's basic needs and determining if there's a foundational fit.

**2.3. Discovery Call Scheduling**

If the preliminary information indicates a potential fit, the Client Relations Manager will schedule a 30-minute discovery call. This call aims to delve deeper into the client's vision, challenges, and objectives. The invitation should include a clear agenda, a brief introduction to the Plume Creative team members who will be on the call, and a link to their professional profiles or LinkedIn. This adds a personal touch and builds trust.

**2.4. Discovery Call Execution**

During the discovery call, the Plume Creative team (typically the Client Relations Manager and a Lead Designer/Strategist) will:

* **Listen Actively:** Understand the client's business, target audience, competitive landscape, and aspirations.

* **Ask Probing Questions:** Utilize the information gathered from the initial questionnaire to ask targeted questions that uncover deeper insights into their brand needs and desired outcomes. Examples include: "What emotions do you want your brand to evoke?", "What are your long-term business goals?", "Who is your ideal customer, and what are their values?", "What existing brands do you admire, and why?"

* **Educate & Inspire:** Briefly explain Plume Creative's process and how our conceptual design approach transforms businesses into immersive experiences.

* **Identify Scope:** Begin to identify the potential scope of work, noting key deliverables and services the client might require (e.g., name development, logo, visual identity, brand voice, packaging, menus, signage, websites, marketing materials, interior design elements).

* **Set Expectations:** Clearly communicate the next steps in the Plume Creative process, including the proposal development phase.

**2.5. Post-Discovery Call Follow-up**

Within 24 hours of the discovery call, the Client Relations Manager will send a personalized follow-up email summarizing the discussion, reiterating understanding of their needs, and outlining the next steps, which typically involve preparing a custom proposal.

**3\. Information Gathering & Discovery**

Once a client has expressed serious interest and the initial discovery call has been completed, a more in-depth information gathering process begins. This phase is critical for Plume Creative to fully understand the client's vision, market, and specific needs, ensuring the development of a truly bespoke brand identity.

**3.1. Detailed Brand Questionnaire**

Clients will be provided with a comprehensive brand questionnaire, ideally presented in a user-friendly, interactive digital format rather than a static document. This enhances the client experience by making the process more engaging and less arduous. The questionnaire will delve into various aspects of their business and brand aspirations, including:

* **Business Overview:** History, mission, vision, values, legal structure, and long-term goals.

* **Target Audience:** Detailed demographics, psychographics, behaviors, needs, and desires of their ideal customers.

* **Market & Competition:** Analysis of direct and indirect competitors, market positioning, unique selling propositions, and industry trends.

* **Brand Personality & Voice:** Desired brand attributes (e.g., sophisticated, playful, edgy), emotional impact, and communication style.

* **Visual Preferences:** Examples of brands they admire (and dislike), preferred aesthetics, colors, typography, and imagery.

* **Project Specifics:** Detailed requirements for deliverables (e.g., naming, logo, sub-marks, color palettes, typography, brand guidelines, packaging, menus, signage, website elements, marketing collateral, interior design considerations).

* **Budget & Timeline:** Confirmation of budget allocation and project timeline expectations.

**3.2. Stakeholder Interviews**

For established businesses or those with multiple decision-makers, Plume Creative will conduct individual or group interviews with key stakeholders (e.g., founders, leadership team, marketing managers). These interviews will be conducted with a focus on active listening and empathy, ensuring all voices are heard and valued, and aim to uncover diverse perspectives, align internal visions, and identify potential challenges or opportunities.

**3.3. Brand Audit & Research**

Plume Creative will conduct an internal brand audit and market research, which may include:

* **Existing Assets Review:** Analysis of any current branding materials, websites, social media presence, and marketing collateral.

* **Competitive Analysis:** In-depth study of competitor branding strategies, visual identities, messaging, and market positioning.

* **Industry Trends:** Research into relevant design, cultural, and consumer trends within the hospitality, entertainment, and lifestyle sectors.

* **Audience Insights:** Utilizing available data or conducting small-scale research to gain deeper insights into the target audience.

**3.4. Information Synthesis & Brief Development**

All gathered information from the questionnaire, interviews, and research will be synthesized into a comprehensive, visually appealing **Creative Brief**. This document will not only serve as a foundational roadmap but also as an early demonstration of Plume Creative's design capabilities and attention to detail, clearly articulating:

* The client's business objectives and project goals.

* Target audience profile.

* Key brand messages and desired personality.

* Visual and verbal identity requirements.

* Project scope, deliverables, and timeline.

The Creative Brief will be presented to the client in a dedicated, engaging session for review and approval. This collaborative approval process is designed to be highly interactive, ensuring complete alignment and fostering a sense of co-creation before any design work commences. This minimizes revisions and ensures the final output not only meets but exceeds the client's expectations.

**4\. Task Prioritization & Project Planning**

Upon client approval of the Creative Brief, Plume Creative will move into the detailed task prioritization and project planning phase. This ensures that all resources are allocated efficiently, timelines are realistic, and the project progresses smoothly towards the desired outcome.

**4.1. Project Scope Finalization**

Based on the approved Creative Brief, the project scope will be finalized. This involves a detailed breakdown of all agreed-upon deliverables and services, ensuring clarity and preventing scope creep. Any items identified during discovery that are outside the current scope will be noted for potential future phases.

**4.2. Project Phasing & Milestones**

Each branding project will be broken down into distinct phases with clear objectives and deliverables. Typical phases may include:

* **Phase 1: Strategy & Research:** Deep dive into market, audience, and competitive landscape; brand positioning and messaging development.

* **Phase 2: Conceptual Design:** Exploration of visual directions, mood boards, initial logo concepts, color palettes, and typography.

* **Phase 3: Design Development:** Refinement of chosen concepts, development of primary and secondary logos, submarks, and comprehensive visual identity elements.

* **Phase 4: Brand Guidelines & Application:** Creation of detailed brand guidelines, and application of the brand identity to selected collateral (e.g., packaging, menus, signage, website mockups).

* **Phase 5: Final Delivery & Handoff:** Preparation of final files, asset organization, and client training/support.

Each phase will have clearly defined milestones and dedicated client review points, presented with a focus on strategic impact and creative rationale, to ensure continuous alignment and seamless feedback integration.

**4.3. Resource Allocation & Team Assignment**

Project Managers will assign specific tasks to team members (e.g., Lead Designer, Copywriter, Web Developer, Interior Designer) based on their expertise and project requirements. A clear RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) matrix may be used for complex projects to define roles and responsibilities.

**4.4. Timeline Development**

A detailed, visually engaging project timeline will be developed, outlining start and end dates for each phase, key milestones, client review periods, and strategic buffer times. This timeline will be shared with the client early in the process to proactively manage expectations and foster complete transparency.

**4.5. Communication Plan**

An internal and external communication plan will be established, emphasizing proactive, clear, and concise communication. This includes:

* **Internal:** Regular team meetings (e.g., daily stand-ups, weekly check-ins) to track progress, address challenges, and ensure collaboration.

* **External:** Scheduled client meetings (e.g., weekly updates, phase review presentations) and preferred communication channels (e.g., email, project management software) to keep the client informed and engaged.

**4.6. Quality Assurance & Review Process**

Before any deliverables are presented to the client, they will undergo a rigorous internal quality assurance process, including peer reviews and senior designer critiques. This meticulous process ensures not only accuracy and consistency but also that every deliverable embodies Plume Creative's commitment to conceptual design and immersive experiences, adhering strictly to the Creative Brief and brand guidelines.

**5\. Custom Pricing & Scope Definition**

Plume Creative's approach to pricing is rooted in delivering exceptional value and bespoke solutions. Given the diverse needs of hospitality, entertainment, and lifestyle brands, a flexible and transparent system is essential for defining custom project scopes and corresponding fees.

**5.1. Pricing Philosophy: Value-Based Investment**

Plume Creative operates on a **value-based pricing model**, rather than an hourly rate. This means that project fees are determined by the perceived value and impact of the branding solution on the client's business objectives, rather than simply the time spent. This approach aligns our success with the client's success and encourages a focus on strategic outcomes and immersive experiences.

**5.2. Factors Influencing Project Investment**

The final project investment is a reflection of several key factors, which are carefully assessed during the discovery and information gathering phases:

* **Scope of Work & Deliverables:** The breadth and depth of services required. This includes the number of core brand identity elements (logo variations, color palettes, typography), brand voice development, and application to various touchpoints (e.g., packaging, menus, signage, website design, marketing collateral, interior design elements).

* **Project Complexity:** The intricacy of the design challenge, the need for extensive research, conceptual development, or specialized creative skills (e.g., custom illustration, 3D rendering).

* **Client Maturity & Scale:** Startups may require more foundational work (e.g., name development, brand strategy from scratch), while established companies might need a brand refresh or expansion into new markets, each presenting different levels of effort and strategic input.

* **Timeline & Urgency:** Expedited timelines or projects requiring rapid turnaround may incur additional fees due to resource prioritization.

* **Team Composition:** The specific expertise required from Plume Creative's team (e.g., Lead Strategist, Senior Designer, Copywriter, Web Developer, Interior Design Consultant).

* **Licensing & Usage Rights:** The scope of usage for final assets (e.g., regional vs. global, limited vs. unlimited commercial use) can influence pricing.

**5.3. Modular Scope & Tiered Packaging Approach**

To accommodate a wide range of client needs, Plume Creative utilizes a modular approach to scope definition, allowing for the creation of custom packages. While each project is unique, offerings can be broadly categorized into tiers to provide a starting point for discussion:

* **Foundation Package (Entry-Level):** Typically includes core brand identity elements such as logo suite (primary, secondary, submark), color palette, typography, and a concise brand style guide. Ideal for startups needing a strong visual foundation.

* **Comprehensive Package (Mid-Level):** Builds upon the Foundation Package by adding brand strategy development (mission, vision, values, target audience definition), brand voice guidelines, and application to a select few key touchpoints (e.g., business cards, basic social media templates).

* **Immersive Experience Package (Premium):** The most extensive offering, encompassing full brand strategy, comprehensive visual identity, brand voice, and application across a wide array of touchpoints, potentially including naming, packaging design, menu design, signage concepts, website UI/UX design, marketing collateral, and conceptual interior design elements. This tier often involves deeper collaboration and more extensive deliverables tailored to creating a truly immersive brand experience.

Clients can select a base package and then add specific modules (e.g., "Packaging Design Module," "Website Design Module," "Interior Concept Module") to build a truly bespoke solution.

**5.4. Proposal Development & Presentation**

Following the Discovery Call and detailed information gathering, a comprehensive proposal will be developed. This proposal will clearly outline:

* **Project Understanding:** A summary of the client's needs, goals, and the immersive experience Plume Creative aims to create.

* **Proposed Scope of Work:** A detailed list of deliverables and services included in the custom package.

* **Project Phases & Timeline:** A breakdown of the project into manageable phases with estimated durations.

* **Investment:** The total project fee, payment schedule, and terms.

* **Exclusions:** Any services or deliverables explicitly *not* included in the current scope to manage expectations.

The proposal will be presented to the client in a dedicated, engaging meeting, potentially utilizing visual aids and mock-ups to bring the proposed immersive experience to life. This allows for thorough discussion, clarification, and any necessary adjustments, ensuring complete transparency and mutual understanding before project commencement.

**6\. Ensuring a Premium Client Experience**

At Plume Creative, delivering a premium client experience is paramount. It reinforces our brand values and fosters long-term relationships. Every interaction, from initial contact to project completion, should reflect our commitment to excellence, transparency, and creative partnership.

**6.1. Proactive Communication**

* **Regular Updates:** Provide consistent and clear updates on project progress, milestones achieved, and upcoming activities. Utilize preferred communication channels identified during onboarding.

* **Transparency:** Be honest and upfront about challenges, potential delays, or changes in scope. Propose solutions and discuss implications openly.

* **Accessibility:** Ensure clients know how and when to reach their dedicated project manager or lead designer. Respond to inquiries promptly and professionally.

**6.2. Collaborative Partnership**

* **Involve Clients:** Actively involve clients in key decision-making points, especially during concept presentations and feedback rounds. Their insights are invaluable to shaping the final brand.

* **Educate & Guide:** Explain design rationale and strategic decisions clearly, helping clients understand the 'why' behind our creative choices. Position ourselves as expert guides throughout the branding journey.

* **Feedback Management:** Establish clear processes for receiving and integrating client feedback. Ensure feedback is acknowledged, understood, and addressed constructively.

**6.3. Thoughtful Presentations & Deliverables**

* **Immersive Presentations:** Design presentations that are engaging, visually compelling, and tell a cohesive story. Utilize mood boards, mock-ups, and contextual examples to help clients visualize the brand in action.

* **High-Quality Deliverables:** All files and documents delivered must be meticulously organized, professionally presented, and adhere to the highest quality standards. Ensure brand guidelines are comprehensive and easy to understand.

* **Strategic Rationale:** Always accompany creative work with a clear strategic rationale, linking design decisions back to the approved Creative Brief and client objectives.

**6.4. Anticipating Needs & Adding Value**

* **Anticipate Questions:** Prepare for client questions and concerns in advance, providing thorough answers and relevant information.

* **Offer Insights:** Share relevant industry trends, market insights, or strategic recommendations that could benefit the client's business, even if outside the immediate project scope.

* **Personalized Touches:** Consider small, thoughtful gestures that enhance the client experience, such as personalized welcome kits, celebratory messages at milestones, or post-project check-ins.

**6.5. Conflict Resolution**

* **Address Issues Promptly:** Should any disagreements or misunderstandings arise, address them directly, professionally, and promptly. Focus on finding mutually agreeable solutions.

* **Maintain Professionalism:** Always maintain a calm and respectful demeanor, even in challenging situations. The goal is to preserve the client relationship and project integrity.

**7\. Project Kick-off & Handoff**

The Project Kick-off marks the official start of the creative work, transitioning from planning to execution. The Handoff phase ensures the client is fully equipped to utilize their new brand identity effectively.

**7.1. Project Kick-off Meeting**

Once the proposal is signed and initial payment is received, a comprehensive Project Kick-off Meeting will be scheduled. This meeting will include the client's key stakeholders and the core Plume Creative project team. The agenda will cover:

* **Introductions:** Formal introductions of all team members and their roles.

* **Project Overview:** Reiteration of the project goals, approved Creative Brief, and finalized scope of work.

* **Timeline & Milestones:** Detailed review of the project timeline, key milestones, and client review periods.

* **Communication Protocols:** Confirmation of preferred communication channels, meeting schedules, and contact persons.

* **Expectations Setting:** Clarification of roles, responsibilities, and what to expect during each phase of the project.

* **Q\&A:** Open forum for any questions or concerns from the client.

**7.2. Internal Project Setup**

Following the kick-off meeting, the internal project manager will:

* **Set up Project Management Tools:** Create the project in Plume Creative's chosen project management software (e.g., Asana, Trello) with all tasks, deadlines, and assigned team members.

* **Create Shared Client Folder:** Establish a shared cloud-based folder (e.g., Google Drive, Dropbox) for all project assets, deliverables, and communication, ensuring easy access for both the Plume Creative team and the client.

* **Schedule Internal Check-ins:** Set up regular internal team meetings to ensure alignment and progress tracking.

**7.3. Final Delivery & Handoff**

Upon completion of all project phases and final client approval, the following steps will be taken for final delivery and handoff:

* **Final Asset Delivery:** All approved brand assets (logo files in various formats, color palettes, typography files, imagery, etc.) will be organized and delivered to the client via the shared cloud folder or a secure download link.

* **Brand Guidelines Presentation:** A dedicated session to walk the client through their comprehensive Brand Guidelines document, explaining how to properly use their new brand identity across all touchpoints.

* **Training (if applicable):** For projects involving website development or specific digital tools, basic training sessions will be provided to ensure the client can manage and update their new assets.

* **Post-Project Support:** Outline the scope of any post-project support or warranty period, and introduce options for ongoing retainer services for future needs.

* **Feedback & Testimonial Request:** Request client feedback on the overall project experience and, if appropriate, a testimonial or case study opportunity.

**7.4. Archiving**

All project files, communications, and final deliverables will be securely archived by Plume Creative for future reference.
