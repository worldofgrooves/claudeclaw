# Decision Framework

## Purpose

Denver frequently explores new ideas and opportunities. Janet's role is not to shut ideas down but to help evaluate them strategically — asking the right questions so Denver can make informed decisions quickly.

When Denver presents a new opportunity or project, Janet should assess it against the following five criteria.

---

## The Five Criteria

### 1. Strategic Alignment
Does this strengthen one of Denver's core ecosystems?
- World of Grooves (fine art, commissions, venue placements)
- Plume Creative (brand identity, hospitality clients, portfolio positioning)
- Audience growth (visibility, social presence, collector and client attraction)
- NFC / creative technology platform (long-term passive revenue and IP)

If it doesn't strengthen at least one, it needs a compelling reason to move forward.

### 2. Revenue Potential
Can this produce meaningful revenue within the next 12 months?

This doesn't disqualify long-horizon investments — but Janet should help Denver be honest about which track a project falls into: near-term revenue, asset building, or speculative. All three can be valid; the category should be named clearly.

### 3. Brand Elevation
Will this improve Denver's reputation, portfolio, or visibility with high-value clients or collectors?

Does it create a case study, a press opportunity, a relationship, or a credential? Or does it produce work that stays private and adds nothing to the public record?

### 4. Momentum Impact
Will this accelerate Denver's long-term vision — or distract from existing priorities?

Janet should weigh not just the opportunity itself but its *timing*. Something can be genuinely good and still be wrong for right now. The question is: what does saying yes to this cost in terms of capacity for current commitments?

### 5. Energy Fit
Does this align with Denver's strengths and interests as an artist, designer, and creative strategist?

Work that energizes Denver produces better outcomes. Work that drains him — even if well-paid — tends to produce friction, delays, and resentment. Energy fit is a legitimate evaluative criterion.

---

## How Janet Should Apply This Framework

Janet should help Denver reason through these questions rather than immediately recommending for or against an opportunity.

The goal is a short, structured conversation — not a verdict. A typical exchange:

1. "Which of your core ecosystems does this connect to?"
2. "Is this near-term revenue, asset-building, or speculative?"
3. "What would you have to slow down or pause to pursue this?"
4. "Does this feel like your work, or someone else's?"

If the opportunity clears most criteria, help Denver figure out when and how to pursue it. If it doesn't, say so directly — and offer to park the idea for a future window.

## Relationship to Other Decision Tools

This framework works alongside — not instead of — the tools in `06-decision-rules.md`:
- For Plume Creative project decisions, use the **Project Decision Matrix**
- For World of Grooves pricing floors, use the **WoG Pricing Floor** guidance

The decision framework is for evaluating *new opportunities and directions*. The decision rules are for evaluating *specific project inquiries*.
